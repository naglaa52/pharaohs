TutAnkhamun

Tutankhamun is one of the pharaohs of the 18th Egyptian dynasty in ancient Egypt's history. He was the Pharaoh of Egypt from 1334 BC to 1325 BC. Tutankhamun is one of the most famous pharaohs, the most prominent being the discovery of his tomb and its treasures intact without any damage. The mystery surrounding his death, with many considering the death of the pharaoh at a very young age to be an unnatural occurrence, especially with evidence of fractures in his thigh and skull bones, his minister's marriage to his widow after his death, and his appointment as pharaoh. All these mysterious events, and the heavy use of the legend of the Pharaoh's curse associated with Tutankhamun's tomb, which was used in movies and video games, made Tutankhamun the most famous pharaoh for puzzles and unanswered questions, some of which are considered one of the oldest assassinations in human history. Tutankhamun died young and was buried in his tomb, tomb 62, in the Valley of the Kings. Tutankhamun was nine years old when he became the Pharaoh of Egypt, and his name in ancient Egyptian means "the living image of the god Amun," the chief god of ancient Egypt. Tutankhamun lived in a transitional period in ancient Egypt's history, where he came after Akhenaten, who attempted to unify the gods of ancient Egypt into the form of one god. During his reign, the return to the worship of the multiple gods of ancient Egypt was established. His tomb was discovered in 1922 in the Valley of the Kings by the British archaeologist Howard Carter, and this discovery caused a widespread media frenzy around the world.

TutAnkhamun's Golden Mask

The golden mask of Tutankhamun is displayed at the Egyptian Museum in Cairo. New research suggests that the famous pharaoh suffered from numerous ailments, and probably spent much of his life in pain before dying at 19 from the combined effects of malaria and a broken leg.






Tomb of Tutankhamun

The tomb of Tutankhamun, located in the Valley of the Kings in Luxor, Egypt, is a relatively small tomb that was discovered nearly intact in 1922 by British archaeologist Howard Carter. The tomb contained over 5,000 artifacts, including Tutankhamun's famous golden death mask, chariots, weapons, and furniture, and provided an unprecedented glimpse into the life, culture, and beliefs of ancient Egyptians. The walls of the tomb are decorated with intricate carvings and paintings that depict scenes from the Book of the Dead and various gods and goddesses from ancient Egyptian mythology.

Valley of the Kings

The Valley of the Kings is a complex of tombs in the Theban Hills of Luxor, Egypt. It was used as a royal burial site for pharaohs of the New Kingdom period, including Tutankhamun, Ramses II, and Tuthmosis III. The tombs are known for their elaborate decorations and hieroglyphics, which offer a glimpse into ancient Egyptian beliefs about the afterlife.
