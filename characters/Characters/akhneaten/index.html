The Head of Akhenaten is a well-known ancient Egyptian artifact that is believed to date back to the 18th dynasty of the New Kingdom period, specifically to the reign of Pharaoh Akhenaten (1353-1336 BCE). The head is made of sandstone and is a fragment of a larger statue. It measures approximately 30 cm in height and depicts the pharaoh with his distinctive features, including a long, thin face, elongated head, and almond-shaped eyes. The nose, lips, and ears of the head are damaged, but the remaining features suggest a highly stylized representation of the pharaoh
