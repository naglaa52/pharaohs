The Statue of Hatshepsut is a well-known ancient Egyptian sculpture depicting the female pharaoh Hatshepsut, who ruled Egypt during the 18th dynasty in the 15th century BCE. The statue is made of red granite and stands approximately 8 meters tall. It portrays Hatshepsut in the traditional pharaonic regalia, including the nemes headdress and false beard, and with crossed arms holding the royal insignia.
