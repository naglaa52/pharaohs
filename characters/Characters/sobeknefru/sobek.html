The Bust of Sobekneferu is an ancient Egyptian sculpture dating back to the 12th dynasty in the 18th century BCE. It is a limestone bust of the pharaoh Sobekneferu, who was one of the few female pharaohs to rule Egypt. The bust stands approximately 50 cm tall and depicts Sobekneferu with a serene expression and wearing a Nemes headcloth with a uraeus cobra. She is also adorned with a broad collar necklace and a pleated garment covering her shoulders. The sculpture is notable for its delicate and finely detailed carving, especially in the facial features .

Sobekneferu or Neferusobek (Ancient Egyptian: Sbk-nfrw meaning 'Beauty of Sobek') was a pharaoh of ancient Egypt and the last ruler of the Twelfth Dynasty of the Middle Kingdom. She ascended to the throne following the death of Amenemhat IV, possibly her brother or husband, though their relationship is unproven. Instead, she asserted legitimacy through her father Amenemhat III. Her reign lasted 3 years, 10 months, and 24 days, according to the Turin King List. She adopted the full royal titulary, distinguishing herself from prior female rulers.
